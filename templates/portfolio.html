About me

Currently a Product Designer for enterprise and consumer web applications at Hightail, I am also a graduate of the University of Michigan School of Information. I have a Master of Science in Information with specializations in Human-Computer Interaction and Information Economics.

I have an enduring fascination with people and their intersection with technology. My current interests include applications related to pervasive (aka ubiquitous) computing and computer-supported cooperative work.

What I enjoy most is digging deeply into the lives of people and applying my creativity to create elegant solutions.

If you’d like to learn more about me, take a look at my Resume/Projects page or My Education.

If you have any other questions, feel free to tweet at me @morgankeys or email me using that same username @gmail.com.

Pervasive Interaction and Experience Design (Spring 2011)

Worked with a team to design and prototype a pervasive (ubicomp) application for the University of Michigan’s brand-new North Quad Residential complex. Used contextual inquiry and “speed-dating” techniques to understand users and the physical environment. The project required us to design for an ecosystem of wall-displays, smartphones, and web-apps. Presented to community directors and developed concept videos (see below for final video). Final prototype featured live QR-codes and semi-responsive adaptive text.

See http://si612boop.wordpress.com/ for more information, including concept description, research, and more!

Computer-Supported Cooperative Work (Fall 2010)

Image by Claire Cabrera


Interviewed subjects and performed work-analysis of employees at University of Michigan’s Business Engagement Center; team project. Produced a report analyzing their transition to a new database/IT infrastructure and provided recommendations.

Interface and interaction Design – “Fordcast” (Fall 2010)

Designed a concept for an audio-based in-car social networking application; team project. Employed methods of contextual inquiry, the affinity-wall method of analysis, and lo-fi and hi-fi prototyping. Submitted a write-up to the CHI 2011 student design competition.

Usability Tester for the Dev-TE@M at UM’s School of Education (Fall/Spring 2010)

Planned and implemented an exploratory usability test plan and improvised remote usability-testing procedures. Improvised a python script for reading and processing access-logs. Worked independently, with little direction or resources, and was able to produce meaningful recommendations.

Internship with Intel: Human Factors Engineer (Summer 2010)

Usability study on the “Circuit” employee web-portal, a home-page for the majority of Intel’s 80,000+ employees. Collaborated with mentor to conduct web-based card-sorting exercises for 340+ users. Conducted 25+ user interviews; produced affinity notes and delivered observations. Designed and lead group participatory-design sessions.

Eval. of Systems and Services – Usability study of the CAMERA Project Web Portal (Spring 2010)

Conducted usability studies with tools such as interaction maps; team project. Interviewed users, created surveys, developed usability tests, and did heuristic evaluation. Culminated in recommendation report. Client reported that more than 80% of recommendations were implemented.

Alternative Spring-Break (Spring 2010)

Volunteered for the Council for the Advancement and Support of Education to write a usability test-plan for improving their website. (sample available on request) I co-wrote a report covering four low-cost usability/design methods–I wrote sections on Heuristic Evaluation and Card-Sorting sections, as well as the Introduction, Conclusion, and Executive Summary.

Contextual Inquiry and Project Mgmt. – Analysis for The Haiti Nursing Foundation (Fall 2009)

Analyzed specific work-processes; team project. Made affinity diagrams, interviewed subjects, created flow, cultural, physical, artifact, and sequence models, created fish-bone causal models, wrote recommendation reports.

Fundamentals of Human Behavior (Fall 2009)

Introductory HCI class at the University of Michigan’s School of Information. Examined the connection between principles in psychology and sociology and good interface design. Studied topics from the sensory-motor system to emotional design and social cognition.
